PROJECT: Delays_Are_Not_Random
TITLE: Data Sources & Feature Engineering

--------------------------------------------------
OVERVIEW:
This dataset was built from a real industrial logistics challenge, integrating variables from planning, shipment management, operational execution, and workforce-related records.

The objective is to estimate delivery delay probability based on factors that directly affect logistics performance.

--------------------------------------------------
DATA SOURCES AND VARIABLE ORIGIN:

1. PLANNING / ROUTING SYSTEM
Variables:
- distance_km
- num_stops
- route_type

Taken from:
Route planning and dispatch records, where each shipment is assigned a route structure before execution.

Reason:
These variables define route complexity. Longer distances, higher stop counts, and route category directly influence exposure to delay.

--------------------------------------------------
2. ERP / SHIPMENT MANAGEMENT
Variables:
- weight_kg
- priority

Taken from:
Shipment master data and order execution records linked to each delivery.

Reason:
These variables describe load characteristics and service importance. Shipment weight affects handling conditions, while priority influences scheduling and operational attention.

--------------------------------------------------
3. OPERATIONS / EXECUTION RECORDS
Variables:
- loading_time_min
- dock_wait_time_min
- departure_shift

Taken from:
Warehouse loading logs, dock utilization records, and shipment release timestamps.

Reason:
These variables reflect execution performance at origin. Loading duration and dock waiting time reveal operational bottlenecks, while departure shift captures different traffic and execution conditions between day and night operations.

--------------------------------------------------
4. WORKFORCE / OPERATIONAL ASSIGNMENT RECORDS
Variables:
- operator_experience_yrs

Taken from:
Operator assignment records and workforce experience history associated with shipment handling activities.

Reason:
Operator experience influences execution stability, handling consistency, and response quality during loading and dispatch.

--------------------------------------------------
5. HISTORICAL DELIVERY PERFORMANCE
Variables:
- delayed

Taken from:
Historical shipment outcome records based on actual delivery performance against expected schedule.

Reason:
This is the target variable. It indicates whether the shipment was delayed and is used as the outcome for predictive classification.

--------------------------------------------------
VARIABLE-BY-VARIABLE SUMMARY:

distance_km
Taken from:
Route planning records
Why it was included:
Longer travel distances increase transit exposure and delay risk.

weight_kg
Taken from:
Shipment master / ERP records
Why it was included:
Load weight affects handling, loading, and transportation conditions.

num_stops
Taken from:
Routing and dispatch planning
Why it was included:
More stops increase route complexity and delay probability.

priority
Taken from:
Order or shipment priority classification
Why it was included:
Priority level affects execution sequence and service handling.

loading_time_min
Taken from:
Loading operation logs
Why it was included:
Longer loading times can create schedule deviation from the origin.

dock_wait_time_min
Taken from:
Dock allocation and queue records
Why it was included:
Represents congestion and operational waiting losses.

operator_experience_yrs
Taken from:
Operator assignment and experience records
Why it was included:
Experience can reduce variability and improve execution consistency.

route_type
Taken from:
Route classification records
Why it was included:
Urban, mixed, and long-haul routes behave differently in terms of risk.

departure_shift
Taken from:
Shipment departure timestamp grouped by operational shift
Why it was included:
Day and night departures are exposed to different traffic and process conditions.

delayed
Taken from:
Historical delivery compliance records
Why it was included:
Defines the outcome variable for the model.

--------------------------------------------------
FEATURE ENGINEERING LOGIC:

The dataset integrates route structure, shipment characteristics, execution timing, and human factors into a single analytical table.

Each variable was selected because it has a direct or indirect relationship with delivery performance and service reliability.

--------------------------------------------------
BUSINESS LOGIC:

Not all shipments have the same probability of delay.

Route complexity, operational waiting time, load conditions, and workforce execution all contribute to logistics variability. Bringing these sources together makes it possible to estimate risk before the delay happens.

--------------------------------------------------
GOAL:

Predict delay probability to:
- Improve OTIF (On Time In Full)
- Prioritize high-risk shipments
- Support better dispatch and execution decisions
- Reduce logistics variability

--------------------------------------------------
NOTE:

The dataset comes from a real industrial logistics challenge and reflects the integration of planning data, ERP shipment records, execution logs, and workforce-related operational information.
